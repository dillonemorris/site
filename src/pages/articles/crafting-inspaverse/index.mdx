I recently created an inspirational quotes app that I named "inspaverse." I wanted to explain my design and development process while building out this side project. That's what my goal is with this post.

## What is inspaverse?

I'm a big fan of reading and of quotes from famous people and authors. A simple one line quote can change your day and make you look at the world a little differently.
I had recently downloaded a mobile app called "Motivation" and very much enjoyed its simplicity.

[Screenshot of Motivation app]

When looking for a new side project to build, I thought it would be a good idea to clone this app, but for the web. In a nutshell, inspaverse is my interpretation of what Motivation might look like in web form, albeit with less features and polish (I'm only 1 developer after all).

[Screenshot of inspaverse]

### Why clone an app?

The idea of cloning an existing app is an idea I picked up when reading swyx's book "The Coding Career Handbook." His idea, an idea shared by many including myself, is that the best way to practice coding is to build projects.
The question then becomes, what do I build? This question, in my experience, is the main barrier between you and a new side project. Deciding to clone an existing app
gets you past this phase seamlessly.

“Making a new app involves a thousand micro decisions - from what the app does, to how it should look, and everything in between. Decide now: Do you want to practice making technical decisions or product decisions? You’re practicing coding, not making a startup. Remember?”

swyx

The idea isn't to make a pixel perfect clone, but to choose an existing product as a jumping off point so you can get to building. In the end, my goal isn't to make money off of this app (at least directly), but to ehance my skills and
learn by doing. You still get to create your own app along the way and make key decisions on how to build it without putting unnecessary pressure on yourself to come up with something grand and novel.
